OpenAI CPA is a distributed automation platform built for high-scale account registration workflows. It combines a high-speed protocol engine with an anti-detection browser engine, orchestrating cluster workers, mail engines, and CPA/Sub2API warehouses to handle thousands of concurrent registrations reliably.

## How it works

OpenAI CPA coordinates multiple subsystems to automate registration at scale. Tasks are dispatched to cluster workers, each running either the Protocol Engine (for raw throughput) or the Browser Engine (for anti-detection). Email verification is handled by the integrated Mail Engine, and completed accounts flow into CPA or Sub2API warehouses.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deploy the cluster">
    Set up your master node and register worker nodes. Workers connect to the master and await task dispatch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your engines">
    Choose the Protocol Engine for maximum throughput or the Browser Engine for anti-detection behavior. Both can run simultaneously across different worker pools.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up the Mail Engine">
    Connect one or more email providers so the platform can poll inboxes and extract verification codes automatically during registration flows.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run registrations and collect accounts">
    Submit registration tasks. Completed accounts land in the CPA Warehouse or are exposed via Sub2API for downstream consumers.
  </Step>
</Steps>
